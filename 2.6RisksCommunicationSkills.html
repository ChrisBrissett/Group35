Risks

In any project large or small, there will always be risks associated. Many of these risks are avoidable if a plan is made and these risks identified early on, often recognised during a risk assessment with a risk/hazard matrix suitable for the tasks.

Thorough research on tools used in our project will alleviate or significantly reduce risk that could lead to project down time, or late delivery. As our project is completed online through collaboration tools such as Microsoft Teams and Discord, we rest easy knowing that the data that we store and enter here is secure and backed up to the cloud consistently. These cloud services are mirrored around the globe, allowing for true redundancy, and ability to work anywhere at any time, even without an internet connection.

As our team is quite young, we are continually developing our teamwork and collaboration skills. We acknowledge that there may be limitations with our reach or the knowledge that we have within the ICT world, but as a team, our wingspan is great. With our 5 members working in very different fields, we are confident with our ability to research tools, and identify what is best for our group and the project at hand.

Some of the consequences that are associated with development of a mobile application are changes to the schedule, unfinished tasks that are assigned to team members, overloading or underloading team members and total re-writes of the app. To mitigate the abovementioned risks, our team will analyse what kind of impact this may have on the team or the project itself. It will allow us as a team to develop strategies to avoid the chance of catastrophe.

One risk that our group aim to avoid for this project is unplanned absences of team members. This leads to delays in completion of individual and group tasks, demotivation, disorganisation and gaps in knowledge when other team members try to pick up the slack. Our team have committed their time to delivering a solid project for assignment 3 and look forward to seeing it grow from what it is today.


Group Processes and Communications

Based on our group and individual reflections from Assignment 2, we have made a few adjustments to our meeting schedule and agendas.
We have elected to meet formally once a week on Tuesday evenings for anywhere from 45mins to an hour, more if everyone is comfortable and we’re making good progress. We do this using Microsoft Teams meetings from there we can share our screens and review our report together. We also have informal catch ups which can last 10-20 minutes on any day we deem its needed, often before a due date, or when members are stuck on what to do next. Frequent text-based communication throughout the week is also encouraged through either discord or Microsoft Teams. If we are unable to contact a team member through Discord or Microsoft Teams, we will send an email to their school address and inform our tutor. Important notes from a meeting or other development they may have missed, will be kept and passed on to them as soon as possible.

Skills and Jobs

Over the next 6 months we aim to have a refined rendition of our app, with both clue-keeping, scorekeeping, and card management available. It will be up on both Google play, and Apple app stores, with plenty of consumer feedback rolling in. In this time as we continue to develop our existing service, we will look into hiring new additions to the team to aid us. This will be especially crucial as we plan to extend our services into other games. The main roles we would be looking to add to our team are as follows.

Project Manager

Our project manager will manage communication between team members, ensuring that everyone is on the same page. This position will involve direct communication with employees as they will organise workflow between staff members, coordinate meetings, and maintain a comfortable work environment. Experience with leadership and management techniques will be needed for this position, as you’ll be taking the head of meetings, and ensuring that everyone completes their work by the specified deadline.

Junior Game Programmer

Our junior game programmer will work in direct support of the Brogrammer. Their role will be to write and implement graphical and functional elements of code into the app. The ability to be self-motivated and have strong technical problem-solving skills will be essential to be able to interact with the already established team. As the development of the app uses C# & C++ languages, are proficiency in these areas will be essential.

Security Engineer

The safety of our consumers’ data is one of our top priorities in Ex Machina. As a Chief Security Engineer, you’ll be in charge of implementing security solutions within our products as you conduct risk assessments and identify/eliminate vulnerabilities in our app. If you have experience with a variety of security products such as firewalls and virus protection, and are ready to work in a fast-paced business environment, this is the job for you.

Creative Director

This is a key position in bringing this project to life, as you'll be the deciding factor in everything from colour palettes to font choice. If you think that image should be shifted a few pixels to the right, then it’s your call to make! You’ll be responsible in setting an appropriate aesthetic to match our vision, ensuring that a high-quality product is produced, that is accessible for all audiences. We’re looking for someone who’s proficient in creative design to elevate our brand; bonus points if you’ve got experience with the Adobe Creative Suite.
